About Me


When it comes to cars, most people don't think about how much the individual parts add up to an overall price tag. Until something goes wrong, that is. Tesla has been dealing with what it calls production bottlenecks at its massive battery factory, called the Gigafactory, near Reno, Nevada. The company went into some detail on the issues during its third-quarter earnings call with analysts, when CEO Elon Musk was at the Gigafactory trying to sort out the slowdown. Battery production for the mass-market Model 3 will be far more automated than it has been for Tesla's previous vehicles, the Model S and the Model X. So will production in general, creating a learning curve that hasn't been smooth for Tesla, as it's harder to fall back on assembling components by hand.
